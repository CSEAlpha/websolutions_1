The Carthaginian Empire

Carthage was a powerful civilization in the ancient Mediterranean, known for its maritime trade, military strength, and conflicts with Rome.

Ethnicity & Culture

The Carthaginians were of Phoenician origin, hailing from the Levant (modern Lebanon). Their culture was a blend of Phoenician, Berber, and Mediterranean influences, excelling in seafaring, commerce, and agriculture.

Wars with Rome

Carthage fought three major wars against Rome, known as the Punic Wars. Despite early successes, such as Hannibal's legendary campaign in Italy, Carthage was ultimately destroyed in 146 BCE after the Third Punic War.

Legacy & Modern Descendants

The people of modern Tunisia are considered the direct descendants of the Carthaginians, with cultural and historical ties still present in the region today.
